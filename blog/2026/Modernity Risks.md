# Modernity Risks

<!-- truncate -->

- Capitalism requires continual growth to sustain. But there are physical limitations of what technology can do.
- Capitalism highly depends on debt which cannot withstand recession.
- Financial industry gets most from monetary expansion. over-financialization.
- Too-big-to-fall financial institutions get bailed out. The profit is private but the risk is moved to whole society. No skin-in-the-game.
- Many jobs are temporary. Once construction finishes, no construction demand for decades. Infrastructure sector face structural job loss. Same applies to AI.
- Automation cannot handle emergency exceptional cases. But automation give no way for human worker to gain expericence for handling emergency cases.
- The half-developed medical techonology often cannot cure some extreme disease but can alleviate disease. but that alleviation is often very expensive. moral standards make we cannot give up patient relatives, comparing there is no "expensive alleviation medicine" in ancient days
- Modern work enviornment punish the responsible workers. Often punish too much on unpredictable failure but reward too few on meaningful work. Then remaining workers focuses on responsibility evasion.
- industrial production is heavy-asset and often face overcapacity. it's not just heavy-asset, the machines are over-specialized unable to adapt to new task. 
  - capital love light-asset industries
  - Supply is rigid. Demand is dynamic. (Shale oil)
